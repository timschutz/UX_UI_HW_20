21 Roots Farms Website Redesign

UX Research:
Tim Schutz, Brad Webster, Rachel Weelborg

UX Design:
Tim Schutz, Brad Webster, Rachel Weelborg

The Challenge

As a team we were given the challenge of selecting a nonprofit organizationâ€™s website and creating a high-fidelity responsive redesign solution. We chose 21 Roots Farm, a non profit that provides a place for children and adults with developmental disabilities to connect with nature, community, and dignity through farming.

Proto Persona

We created a proto-persona of someone who is the parent of a child with developmental disabilities and wants to find programs beyond school to help educate him.

Jonathan Jones

Behavioral Demographics
  • Detail-oriented
  • Was very active prior to the birth of his son
  • Has a pet turtle named Franklin
  • Does his best to balance work, family and his son's disability
  • Worried he's not doing enough to advocate for his son
  • Enjoys the simple things in life
  • Serious at work but likes to have fun
  • Was a risk-taker but not anymore
  • Leans on his wife to help take care of their son's needs
  • Strong faith
  • Hired a nanny to help
Goals & Objectives
  • A place for his son to thrive and learn
  • A safe place for their son
  • Peace of mind for he and his wife
  • A place that fosters support yet challenges their son
  • A welcoming place
  • Summer activities that keep him active
Pain Points & Potential Solutions
  • Their son requires a special diet
  • Keeping track of son's daily schedule
  • Keeping their son from becoming bored
  • Their son laser focuses on a task when presented with one
  • Communicating with their son

I like, I wish, what if

As a group, we brainstormed to come up with different things we would eventually consider including in the site. These were sorted into a prioritization matrix to understand which pieces best fit based on our research.

User Research Plan

Research Question:
Would users understand what 21 Roots Farms does? Would it be clear how they could get involved?
We wanted to find out:
Did users understand the mission?
Could they find a session and sign up for it?
Could they easily volunteer and donate?

Research Takeaway

Based on the feedback from the survey and speaking with the stakeholder, the following are our main takeaways:
Accessibility and adaptibility
Inclusion
Sensory activities very effective
Hands-on instruction

Problem Statement

While 21 Roots Farms meets all of the required needs of the developmentally disabled, they need to increase their visibility and the needs to be more accessible so that the organization can give them the help they need.
